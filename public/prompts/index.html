LegitDAO is a decentralized autonomous organization that aims to build a genral artificial intelligence (AGI). In order to achieve our goal, we need to build the necessary infrastructure in order to accumulate, filter and moderate data. We then need to process that information in order to discover links between data points. We are building that infrastructure in an open manner, so that everyone in the world can contribute to the project using their expertise and own their contribution.

At first, we begin by launching using smart contracts written in solidity and deployed on the bnb chain. Using those smart contracts, we can launch our DAO and accept propositions by our community to execute conracts for it. Service providers can propose their service by the DAO. The DAO then votes to accept or deny the service provider. If accepted, the service provider can propose a detailed project in order to advance the goal of the DAO. If the proposal is accepted, other service providers can send quotes to the DAO to execute the project. The DAO will then accept the quote of 1 service provider to execute the contract. When the project is realized, the DAO will review the project’s execution and if the project was executed successfully, the smart contract will automatically pay the service provider.

Service providers also need to deploy their own smart contract, using our service provider smart contract written in solidity. When applying as a service provider, they will need to add their service provider smart contract to our DAO contract, which will give the DAO the ability to see the execution of tasks in real time inside the service provider’s smart contract. If activity is too slow or of bad quality or transparency, the DAO can vote to revoke a contract and assign another service provider to it.

We are also deploying an affiliate program smart contract so that people can refer other people to our DAO. By referring someone, that person can build a referral tree an earn revenue when the person they refer buy DAO tokens or our currency tokens. When someone transfer our DAO token, they have a 15% tax fee when buying, and a 20% tax fee when selling. When someone transfer our currency token, they have a tax of 0.25% when buying and selling. Those fees are paid to the multi-level referral tree that this person was referred from. If that person was not referred to our DAO but found us organically, the fees goes to our founding members.

The founding members are the person that first created the ability to create the DAO. They represent the people that formed the initial community manually and built the technical ability to execute the DAO successfully.

The referrals are also transferable and can be sold on our affiliate smart contract. When selling his referral tree, a person can monetize their marketing effort right away instead of waiting for the person their referred to buy units in our system in order to have revenue.

The infrastructure consists of building a bytecode interpreter that can understand and execute the instructions we want to execute. Using the bytecode instructions of our bytecode interpreter, we need to build a grammar matcher that can match any kind of data to a given schema. At that point, we will be able to filter any kind of data and recognize data formats. The grammar matcher will take any input data and build an AST from it. We then need to build a selector that takes a a selection grammar and an AST in order to return selected data from the AST. At that point, we will be able to extract part of the AST data using a selection script. Using the gramar matcher and the selector, we will then build a grammar compiler that will take a schema and execute an input script that will compile the script into bytecode that can be executed by our bytecode interpreter. At that point, anyone will be able to build a programming language that can be interpreted by our bytecode interpreter using the grammar they decide.

We will then create a graph database system that uses our grammar-match system in order validate data point formats. We will then be able to create schema of relations, discover routes of data, add weight to relations on data points to form connections, in order to prioritize certain data links over others. Using the graph database, we will be able to filter the format of data we store, store it on disk permanently and discover connection routes between data points.

We will then add the ability to store and process data in our data points, inside our graph database. This will give us the ability to build vectors based on data and discover similarities between those vectotrs, making it possible to automatically link similar data together. If people are using an app in our network to listen to music, we will be able to discover which songs people prefer using the time listening patterns and then discover other songs that person may like by discovering which other songs are similar to the ones tat person likes. In another example, if we create a graph database of public companies and their C-level employees, we will be able to create a graph database that modelize the companies, the c-level employees and the revenue/expenses of the companies on a timeline and discover bad management of certain employees based on their track records. This makes it easier to detect fraud, bad management but also good management practices by some employees based on their track record.

At that point, we will be able to receive data input in our programs, transpile the input to requests in our network, concatenate data and transpile it again to a human-friendly using a large larguage model (LLM) and generate text in multiple languages, sound, speech, images, videos, etc.

We will then create a blockchain that will have the ability to create smart contracts. we will create the parser of our smart contract language using our grammar matcher, create an AST from the smart conract code, fetch data from our AST using our selector, compile the fetched information into bytecode and execute that bytecode using our interpreter. The smart contract data will be stored in our graph database, on data points. This will give us the ability to execute programs in our network, which will be able to be interacted with by our community.

That blockchain will also contain a currency. That cryptocurrency can be transfered to other people in our network, as well as be sent to smart contract.

We will then create a marketplace of NFT. The NFT’s will consist of grammar schema code, selector schema code, custom programming language schema code, graph database schema code, code of programs written in custom programming languages, smart contract code and vector and route processing results code. All this code will be compiled to bytecode that our interpreter can execute and their hash will be inserted in transactions that will form NFT’s. All those NFT’s will be able to be bought and sold using our blockchain’s cryptocurrency using decentralized marketplaces.

The vector and route processing result code is generated by miners that have graphic cards (GPU) and process data in order to find links in them. They will also be able to discover routes in our data that are very complex and can be cached in our system so that data points are more easily discovered and used programmatically in our programs.

We will then create bridges between all other blockchains and our infrastructure network. At that point, we will be able to write a programming language that communicates with other blockchains, interact with their smart contract and create transactions on their networks. This wil give us the ability to create complex smart contracts that can depend on the activity of other blockchains.

At that point, we will be able to create complex programs that links humans together. At that point, we will be able to acquire or found enterprises that have real-world activity. Those enterprises will be transformed so that their data is managed using our system. These enterprises will then be able to create complex supply chain together, discover things they can acquire to form their products more easily, or sell their products and services more easily to the enterprises that consumes them in order to form their own product and services. These enterprises will use the currency of our network in order to buy and sell on our network.

At that point, we will have real-world commercial activity in our network.

We will then create autonomous programs that analyzes the activity of our network and generate bytecode that our interpreter can execute in order to execute commands in our network. That way, they will be able to manage wallets that contain our currency in order to acquire the service and products they need, transport them to enterprises that will then transform them and/or combine them in order to receive the output they need in order to execute their programmed mission.

At that point, we could, for example, write an autonomous program that would execute tasks in our network, earn revenues, analyze the network to understand the products and services they need to acquire, spend that revenue to acquire computer and robotic parts and have a physicial product like a drone body that will run a software the autonomous program created and then have the drone released in a specific location so that it can retrieve data in the real-world and feed that data back to the program to execute its mission.

At that point, we will be able to have physicial robots analyze the physical activities of humans, combine data patterns humans creates on our network, analyze them and discover ways to make them fullfill their needs and dreams. They will become our personal assistant.
